Hierarchical Faceting

Hierarchical facets gives users the ability to perform a search and then drill down into more specific aspects of the data. Typically, hierarchical faceting is achieved in Solr using a small number of predefined categories (for example, Amazon-style genres).

When data is richly annotated to ontologies, we have much more power for categorization, but simply converting to ontology to a tree often doesn't work well - upper classes are often quite abstract, and frequently a user will have to drill down through many levels to get to the terms they require.

In BioSolr, we have been looking for ways to use data and ontologies hand in hand to dynamically predict categorisations based on the results of a search. A few examples of what can be done are shown on this page.

Search Demo

The example below uses some dummy data from the GWAS Catalog, which is richly annotated to the Experimental Factor Ontology. This provides a search facility over GWAS publications. You can search by text that appears in the publication title, or ontology terms that these publications have been annotated to. Searches that hit parent terms will be expanded to children automatically. On the left, several facets are presented that categorize your search results using the ontology in several different ways. Feel free to play around with them!
